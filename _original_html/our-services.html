Muscle Tone, Segmental Control, and Proper Alignment
(MTF-Focused Therapy)

At our center, we focus on optimizing Muscle Tone and Function (MTF) to enhance segmental control and achieve proper postural alignment in children with neuromotor challenges.
We assess each child's:
* Muscle tone (hypotonia, hypertonia, spasticity)
* Segmental control (the ability to independently move and stabilize different parts of the body such as the head, trunk, and pelvis)
* Alignment (neutral positioning of head, spine, and limbs)
This approach supports better balance, coordination, and functional movement-key for milestones like crawling, and walking.

Neurodevelopmental Therapy (NDT)

A hands-on, evidence- based approach designed to help children with neurological and developmental conditions such as Cerebral Palsy, Developmental Delays, Down Syndrome, and other motor disorders. NDT focuses on improving posture, movement, and functional abilities by addressing abnormal muscle tone, movement patterns, and coordination. Our goal is to help your child move more efficiently, in Our pediatric rehab center offers a compassionate, child-friendly environment led by experienced therapists who understand both the science and the heart behind neurodevelopmental care. We blend NDT with other therapies like sensory integration, Dynamic Movement Intervention (DMI), and occupational therapy to create a holistic, effective treatment plan.

Brain Gym

Brain Gym is a series of simple, movement-based exercises designed to enhance brain function, coordination, and learning. Originally developed by Dr. Dr. Mudasir Amin, it's often used with children to support focus, memory, balance, and overall cognitive development.

Speech Therapy

Speech therapy helps children and adults improve their communication and language skills. No matter what’s affecting your ability to speak or communicate effectively, speech therapy can improve your quality of life. Sensory Integration Therapy

Sensory integration, or sensory processing, refers to the processing, integration, and organisation of sensory information from the body and the environment. Simply put, this means how we experience, interpret and react to (or ignore) information coming from our senses.
